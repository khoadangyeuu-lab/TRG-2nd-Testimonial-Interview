Welcome Back & Introduction
Welcome back — it's great to see you again. This is your second testimonial interview, so you already know how this works. We're here for an open, honest conversation about how your internship has been progressing. There are no right or wrong answers, just speak freely and share whatever feels true to you.
What we're covering today
Connections How you've been connecting with the team and engaging in TRG's shared activities like Ice Breaking and Knowledge Sharing.
Then…
Expectations & Growth Reflecting on what you came in expecting, how the internship has matched those expectations, and where you want to go from here.
And…
Tasks & Environment The work itself — what's been enjoyable, what's been challenging, and how you're navigating TRG's office environment and time management style.

Part 5 — Expectations
5.1 What were your original expectations from this internship? ▼
If they give a vague answer like "to learn and grow"
Think back to before your first day — what were you specifically hoping you'd be doing, or skills you wanted to pick up?
Was there anything you were nervous wouldn't happen — something you were hoping for but weren't sure you'd get?
Grounding them in the "before" mindset often unlocks much more honest and specific answers than asking in the abstract.
5.2 In what way has this internship met your expectations? ▼
If they give a general positive answer
Can you point to a specific moment or project where you felt — yes, this is exactly what I came here for?
Was there something that went even better than you expected?
5.3 In what way has this internship not met your expectations? ▼
If they hesitate or say "everything's been great"
It's completely fine to be honest here — this isn't a complaint, it's feedback. Was there anything you hoped to do or experience that hasn't come up yet?
Sometimes reality is just different from what we imagine — not worse, just different. Has anything been surprisingly different from what you pictured?
Frame it as "different" rather than "disappointing" — that lowers the stakes and often produces a more genuine answer.
5.4 What do you want to specifically achieve from now to the end of the internship? ▼
If they give a broad answer like "do my best"
If you had to name one concrete thing — a skill, a deliverable, a relationship — what would success look like for you by the final week?
Is there anything specific you want to be able to say you've done when you look back on this experience in a year's time?
Concrete goals make for stronger testimonial content — help them land on something specific rather than generic.
Part 6 — Career Path
6.1 Have you done anything so far during the internship that will influence your future career direction? ▼
If they say "I'm not sure yet"
Even a small nudge counts — have you tried something here and thought "I really enjoy this" or, on the flip side, "this isn't for me"?
Has meeting people at TRG — seeing what they do day-to-day — given you any new ideas about possible paths?
Removing the expectation of a grand revelation makes it easier for them to identify the smaller, real moments that actually do shape direction.
6.2 As a result of your internship so far, have you gained more clarity over your future career direction, and if so what? ▼
If they say "a bit more" or "not really"
Sometimes clarity looks like narrowing things down — are there things you've ruled out, or areas you now know you want to explore further?
Before this internship, how sure were you about what you wanted to do? And where would you put yourself on that scale now?
Ruling things out IS clarity — make sure they know that counts too. It's just as valuable as finding a definitive answer.
Part 7 — 3 Words
7.1 Which 3 words would you use to describe TRG as a company? ▼
If they freeze or can't choose
Just say whatever comes to mind first — you can always swap out a word. There's no wrong answer here.
Think about your gut feeling walking into work — how would you describe the energy or atmosphere?
This question works best when you let the silence breathe — resist filling it. Their first instinct is usually the most authentic.
7.2 Which 3 words would you use to describe your internship experience so far? ▼
To go deeper after they share their words
Take one of those words — which one feels most true? Can you tell me a quick story or moment that explains why you picked it?
Would those words have been different if you'd answered this question on your very first week?
Asking them to unpack just one word gets a richer, more memorable answer than asking about all three at once.
Part 8 — Tasks
8.1 What's the most enjoyable thing you've done so far? ▼
If they mention a task or project
What specifically made it enjoyable — was it the challenge, the outcome, the collaboration, or something else?
Is this something you'd want to do more of — either here or in a future role?
8.2 What's the hardest thing you've done so far? ▼
If they say "everything was hard at first" or give a generic answer
Think of a specific task or moment where you thought — I'm really not sure how to get through this. What happened?
How did you eventually work through it — did someone help, or did you figure it out yourself?
The "how you got through it" part is often the most compelling part of the story — make sure you get to it.
8.3 How do you feel about doing a task incorrectly? ▼
If they say "I feel bad" or "I hate making mistakes"
That's completely natural — has there been a specific moment here where you made a mistake and how did you handle it?
How did those around you — your manager or teammates — react? Did that help or make it harder?
Has your attitude towards mistakes shifted at all since starting here?
This question reveals a lot about their resilience and psychological safety. A follow-up about TRG's reaction tells you as much as their own feelings do.
8.4 What's your most valuable learning so far? ▼
If they mention a technical skill only
Beyond the technical side — is there something about how to work, communicate, or think through problems that you've picked up here?
Is this something you couldn't have learned in a classroom or online? What made learning it here different?
8.5 Anything you do not want to do anymore? ▼
If they're too polite to say anything negative
There's no wrong answer here — honest feedback is genuinely helpful. Is there a type of task where you find yourself losing energy or focus?
Sometimes it's not about the task itself but how it's structured — is there anything you'd do differently if you had more control over it?
Frame it as self-awareness, not complaining — "knowing what drains you is just as useful as knowing what energises you."
8.6 Anything you would want to do but have not had the time or opportunity so far? ▼
If they draw a blank
Think about what other teams at TRG do — is there a department or role you've seen that made you curious?
If you could shadow anyone at TRG for a day, who would it be and why?
The "shadow for a day" question almost always sparks something — it's low-stakes and gets to genuine curiosity.
Part 9 — Office Environment & Time Management
9.1 What challenges have you had in the first few weeks? ▼
If they say "not many" or "it was fine"
Think about the very first week — was there anything that felt overwhelming, confusing, or just took longer to figure out than you expected?
Were the challenges more about the work itself, or navigating the people and the environment?
9.2 How do you feel about TRG's approach to tasks? ▼
If they give a surface-level positive answer
How are tasks typically given to you — and does that style work well for the way you like to work?
Is there a level of independence or structure in how you approach tasks that surprised you — compared to university assignments or other roles?
9.3 How do you feel about TRG's approach to deadlines? ▼
If they say "it's strict" or "it's flexible"
How does that compare to what you were used to at university — has the pressure been more or less than expected?
Has there been a time where a deadline felt tight — and if so, how did you handle it?
9.4 How do you feel about TRG's approach to time management? ▼
If they're unsure what this means
Think about things like flexible hours, how meetings are scheduled, or how people prioritise their day — does TRG's approach suit you?
Has working in a professional environment changed how you think about managing your own time?
9.5 How are you approaching your work as far as time management is concerned? ▼
If they say "I just try to stay on top of things"
What tools or habits are actually helping you — a to-do list, calendar blocking, anything specific?
Are there moments where you feel like time just gets away from you — and what do you do when that happens?
Specific habits and concrete tools make for a much more useful answer than "I just try to stay organised." Gently push for examples.
9.6 How do you feel about your fellow work colleagues? ▼
If they give a general "they're great"
Is there someone here who has particularly stood out for you — whether as a mentor, a collaborator, or just someone who made you feel welcome?
Have your relationships with colleagues grown since you started — do you feel more connected now than you did at the beginning?
9.7 How do you feel about your fellow interns? ▼
If they seem reserved in their answer
Has being part of an intern group made the experience easier — knowing there are others going through the same thing at the same time?
Do you and the other interns support each other outside of work tasks — sharing tips, checking in on each other?
9.8 How do you feel about TRG's flexible working style? ▼
If they haven't experienced much flexibility yet
Even in small ways — things like choosing when to take breaks, how you structure your day, or having some say in your tasks — have those freedoms felt different from what you expected?
Does more flexibility motivate you, or do you find you work better with more structure?
Some people genuinely thrive with structure — validating both answers makes this a much more honest conversation.
9.9 How do you balance your internship with your studies or other commitments? ▼
If they say "it's tough but manageable"
What does a typical day or week look like for you right now — how do you actually split your time?
Has TRG been flexible or understanding when those commitments clash — and has that made a difference?
If you could change one thing about how you're managing everything, what would it be?
